# Design: Hungr — Taste-Calibrated Food Discovery (provenance-first)

Generated by /office-hours on 2026-06-29
Branch: main
Repo: hungr (local, empty repo — initial commit only)
Status: APPROVED
Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement
Finding food you'll actually like is broken because the dominant signal — Google
reviews — is demographic noise. A star rating aggregates strangers whose palate has
no correlation with yours, so a "4.2" means different things for different cuisines
(e.g. Asian food is systematically under-rated by reviewers who didn't grow up eating
it). The founder is a daily foodie who spends 5+ hours/week hunting for food and runs
a manual evaluation funnel (rating → reviews → photos → menu, 3 of 4 steps inside
Google Maps) precisely to work around this. He wants food judged by *people whose taste
matches his* — provenance over popularity — and a personal map that remembers where
he's been, wants to go, and wants to avoid.

## Demand Evidence
- **Founder is the power user:** eats out almost daily, 5+ hrs/week finding food, has a
  detailed manual evaluation funnel. Strongest possible "scratch your own itch" signal.
- **Observed behavior across his network (not hypothetical):**
  - 1 person actively maintains a Google Maps saved-list — and says manual logging is "a pain."
  - 5–6 people (incl. founder) keep food lists in Notes / spreadsheet / camera roll.
  - ~90% of his friends ask friends for recommendations, especially when traveling overseas.
  - Of those, ~70% run on "memory + vibes" — they trust their network *because of
    taste similarity that Google reviews don't provide.* Founder states this as fact, not hunch.
- **Founder's own list is abandoned ~90% of the time** because it lives in Notes (shared
  with his girlfriend) and doesn't translate to the map where decisions actually happen.
  The pain is the *disconnect between the list and the map*, not the absence of a list.

## Status Quo
Open Google Maps → search "food near me" / "[cuisine] near me" → scan star rating
(calibrated mentally by cuisine) → read 5–10 reviews sorted by relevance (sometimes
newest, to catch management changes) → check photos → fall back to the restaurant's
menu site. A parallel hand-kept Notes list exists but is divorced from the map and
mostly abandoned. Cost: hours per week, decision fatigue, repeated "where should we eat"
loops, and no way to suppress cuisines the user dislikes (Google will keep surfacing them).

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge
**Primary user (the engine): the daily foodie** — rare, high-frequency, generates the
content and taste data. Build for this person first; the special-occasion majority
(date nights, groups, travel — high stakes, too low frequency to anchor a habit on)
free-rides on the foodie's curation later (Strava pattern: obsessives post, casuals browse).

**Wedge:** NOT "social food app" (that's the crowded category consensus — see Premises).
The wedge is **provenance + taste calibration**: reviews/picks attributed to *who the
reviewer is* ("Sichuan spots rated by someone who grew up on Sichuan food"), a score
calibrated by palate-match rather than raw average, and a personal go/been/**avoid** map
that suppresses disliked cuisines — something Google structurally cannot do.

## Constraints
- **Solo build.** One developer. Meta-goal: a portfolio-grade showcase of technical
  ability *and* taste, while genuinely solving the founder's own problem.
- **Mobile-first** (decisions happen on phones, at the moment of hunger), web possible later.
- **Google Places API can be the base map/POI layer ONLY.** It legally cannot supply the
  differentiator (see Premises / Open Questions).
- **Geography:** start where the US incumbents are weak — Australian cities — and lean
  into Asian-cuisine authenticity.
- **Framing constraint (T&S/brand):** the identity/provenance feature is correct as a
  *product* but must be framed as opt-in self-identification + "food reviewed by people
  who grew up eating it" — never as "filter reviews by race." Careless framing risks App
  Store rejection and PR damage.

## Premises (all confirmed with the founder)
1. The social-trust / "Strava for food" concept is **not** the differentiator — it's the
   category consensus, already shipped and growing. The defensible edge is **taste-demographic
   calibration + under-served Australia/Asian-food geography.** *(Founder refined this
   further: the wedge isn't social at all — it's authentic-provenance reviews; social is
   just distribution.)* **Competitor set (consolidated):** Beli (US category leader,
   accelerating), Zest (launched June 2026, credit-card auto-import), Truffle (Instagram-
   Stories auto-import), BiteSight (video-first), Eater; and from the founder's own research:
   Corner (no Australia), Mapstr (places/activities, not food-niche).
2. Near-zero logging is **mandatory table-stakes**, not an innovation — competitors already
   auto-import via credit card (Zest) and Instagram Stories (Truffle). A 1-click check-in
   into go/been/avoid is the floor. **v1 intentionally ships below this floor** (founder
   manually writes his own reviews) because v1 is single-player/founder-only — auto-import
   becomes a requirement at v2, when non-founder users arrive.
3. The first user is the **daily foodie (the founder)** — the content engine — not the
   special-occasion majority.
4. A full social network is the wrong *first* build for a solo dev: it dies at cold-start
   (no friends on it = useless). v1 must be **valuable with zero other users** and get
   better socially later.
5. The differentiator depends on **original review supply** (Google can't provide it).
   Cold-start paths: (a) founder + ~50-person circle writing real reviews as a casual
   friends app; (b) curating named local food creators. Founder accepts both; v1 starts
   with self/circle, creators come as the product matures.

## Approaches Considered
### Approach A: "The Map" — single-player taste map
Personal food map; 1-click check-in into go/been/avoid; cuisine-suppression filter;
reviews are the user's own notes. No identity layer, no social. Effort S–M, Risk Low.
Useful day one, zero cold-start, but doesn't yet prove the wedge.

### Approach B: "Provenance" — the full thesis
Identity-attributed reviews (opt-in self-ID), palate-matched calibrated score, creator
curation pipeline, go/been/avoid map, follow-friends social graph. Effort L–XL, Risk High.
The real company, but a brutal solo cold-start.

### Approach C: "Creator Atlas" — curated provenance from day one
Curated map of ONE city's best spots, ranked by named local creators, sliced by cuisine +
reviewer identity; read-first, founder-curated seed; personal go/been/avoid on top.
Effort M, Risk Med. Differentiator live on day one without a chicken-and-egg.

**Terminology — "provenance" means one thing in this doc:** a review/pick is attributed to
*who the reviewer is* (their self-declared cuisine background/expertise), so a user can see
"this Sichuan place rated by someone who knows Sichuan food." Creator-curation (Approach C)
is one *source* of provenance content, not a second meaning of the word.

## Recommended Approach
**Full thesis (B) as the destination, shipped as sequenced versions** (founder's decision):

- **v1 = Approach A** — ship the single-player go/been/avoid map (with cuisine suppression)
  on a Google Places base. Useful to the founder immediately. **What v1 proves:** that the
  founder can build and ship, and that the map replaces his abandoned Notes list (habit +
  shippability) — *not* the wedge. Geography-agnostic: works anywhere Google Places has
  coverage. Founder seeds his own reviews as he eats out.
- **v2a = provenance core** — identity-attributed picks + the taste-calibrated score, proven
  first with founder+circle UGC only (lowest legal risk). **What v2a proves: the wedge.**
- **v2b = Approach C creator seed** — curated content from named local creators for one city
  (Australian/Asian-food focus), once a supply path is legally cleared (Open Question #1).
  Deferred behind v2a so the wedge is validated before the legally-complex creator pipeline.
- **v3+ (unscoped backlog — NOT a committed roadmap)** — social graph (follow friends),
  broader UGC with self-identification, creator onboarding at scale; and speculative:
  OpenTable/EatClub booking links, list-import (Google Maps saved places / spreadsheet),
  monetization experiments.

Rationale: this stages risk correctly for a solo builder — a usable artifact at every
version, the wedge proven (v2a) before the expensive creator/social machinery, and each
version independently demo-able as portfolio evidence.

**Portfolio guardrail:** the showcase value comes from a *shipped, used* artifact — not from
an ambitious stack or premature scale. Resist gold-plating; the win is daily use, not architecture.

**Tech stack is explicitly deferred to engineering review** (founder's instruction: "the
tech stack is super important... invoke the eng skill before writing anything"). Do not
choose a stack here. Key inputs eng review must weigh: mobile-first (native vs.
cross-platform vs. PWA), Google Places API usage limits/cost and ToS compliance,
offline/map performance, and a data model that can carry identity-attributed reviews +
the go/been/avoid/calibrated-score layer from v1 without a rewrite at v2.

## Data Model Sketch (v1, with v2 fields reserved)
Rough entities so the v1 schema is forward-compatible with v2 (eng review to finalize):
- **Place** — `place_id` (Google, the only field safe to persist long-term), cuisine_tags[],
  cached display fields refreshed within Google's retention limits (see Feasibility).
- **CheckIn / Visit** — user_id, place_id, `status: go | been | avoid`, timestamp.
- **Review** — user_id, place_id, text, rating, cuisine, created_at. *(v2 reserve:
  reviewer_identity_ref, calibrated_score inputs.)*
- **User** — id, suppressed_cuisines[]. *(v2 reserve: self-declared cuisine
  background/expertise dimensions — opt-in.)*

This is a sketch, not a spec — but it backs the "no v1→v2 rewrite" claim: the identity and
score fields must be *reserved* in v1 even though they're empty until v2.

## Edge Cases (v1)
- Place with no Google Places match (manual/freeform entry?).
- Place spanning multiple cuisines (multi-tag). **Default rule:** suppress a place only if
  *all* its cuisine tags are suppressed — so suppressing "Thai" never hides a Sichuan/Thai spot.
- Offline at the moment of hunger (read access to saved map must work offline).
- Edit/delete a review or change a place's go/been/avoid status.
- Place closes or changes ownership (founder explicitly tracks "management changes").

## Open Questions
1. **Authentic-review data sourcing — KILL-RISK, not just an open question.** Google's API
   can't supply identity-tagged reviews and forbids building a competing review DB off it.
   The whole thesis (v2+) is gated on a problem with no demonstrated solution. **Before
   committing to v2b, spike ONE concrete supply path to a yes/no** (e.g. a single creator
   partnership with explicit permission + linking, not scraping). If no legal supply path
   exists at solo-dev scale, v2a (founder+circle UGC) may be the ceiling — design accordingly.
2. **Identity/provenance UX & framing.** How users self-identify (opt-in, optional, which
   dimensions), how it's surfaced, and how to keep it authenticity-coded, not race-filter-coded.
   **Lower-risk reframe to evaluate:** base the calibrated score on self-declared *cuisine
   expertise/preference* ("I know Sichuan food") rather than heritage/upbringing — likely
   lower App Store and PR risk. **Tradeoff to validate in design review:** expertise is a
   softer, more gameable signal than heritage (the problem is grounded in "grew up eating it"),
   so it lowers risk at some cost to signal quality. Needs a design review pass.
3. **Taste-calibrated score definition (define before v2a).** Provisional: per-cuisine
   normalized score = a place's rating among reviewers who self-declare expertise in that
   cuisine, weighted toward the requesting user's declared preferences, shown alongside (not
   replacing) the raw Google average. Inputs: reviewer cuisine-identity/expertise, user
   cuisine preferences, per-cuisine rating distributions. **Cold-start reality:** with
   founder+circle UGC only, most places have 0–1 expert reviews, so v2a should prove the
   wedge on a *handful of hand-seeded high-density places* (one cuisine, one neighborhood),
   not city-wide. Eng review to validate the score on sparse data.
4. **Google Places ToS for v1 (feasibility).** Places restricts caching/retention of most
   Place fields (~30 days; `place_id` is the durable exception) and displaying Places data
   outside a Google map. This touches v1's core data model, not just v2 — flag for eng review.
5. **Geography & "avoid" data.** Confirm Google Places cuisine-tag coverage/quality in target
   Australian cities (Places `types` are coarse — may need a richer cuisine taxonomy source).
6. **Monetization** (deferred): AI search, premium search, creator/business promotion, ads.
   Founder's own WTP signal: would pay if search is strong enough.

## Success Criteria
- **v1 (proves habit + shippability):** Founder uses it instead of raw Google Maps for his
  daily "what's good near me," and his go/been/avoid map replaces the abandoned Notes list.
  Measured by: founder's own daily active use for ≥2 weeks. *(Optional stretch: ≥1 of the
  5–6 note-keepers adopts it — only testable once a distribution path exists, e.g. installable
  PWA link or TestFlight; otherwise v1 is founder-only by design.)*
- **v2a (proves the wedge):** A first-time user gets a genuinely better pick than Google's
  top result for at least one cuisine in one hand-seeded high-density neighborhood,
  attributable to provenance. Measured by: 10 people from the founder's circle use it for a
  real meal decision and say it beat their status quo.
- **Portfolio:** each version is independently demo-able and shippable as evidence of the
  founder's technical ability + taste.

## Distribution Plan
Mobile-first → primary channel is an app store (and/or an installable PWA to start, to
avoid review friction during v1). Confirm in eng review: PWA vs. native affects distribution,
cold-start install friction, and Google Maps SDK choices. CI/CD and release pipeline to be
defined with the chosen stack. (Deferred to eng review — do not lock here.)

## Dependencies
- Google Places (Maps) API account + billing + ToS review.
- A tech-stack decision (eng review) before any implementation — founder's explicit gate.
- v2b depends on resolving Open Question #1 (review data sourcing); v2a is viable on circle-UGC alone.
- Social/UGC (v3) depends on v2 traction and a moderation/identity model.

## The Assignment
Before any code: **manually become "v1" for one week.** For every place you eat or consider
this week, write a one-paragraph real review and tag it go / been / avoid in a single doc
or spreadsheet — and note, each time, the *one thing* you wished an app told you that Google
didn't. This does three things: (1) proves to yourself you'll actually generate review
supply (the v2a review-supply risk), (2) produces your seed dataset and validates the Data
Model Sketch above against real entries, and (3) hands engineering review concrete data
instead of a guess. Bring that doc to the eng review.

# Engineering Review — Resolved Architecture (2026-06-29, branch main)

Generated by /plan-eng-review, then REVISED after a Codex outside-voice challenge + deep
legal research. The earlier "live LLM authenticity engine over Google reviews" was RETRACTED
(see below). Decisions in this revised section are LOCKED.

## Resolved: how v1 sources "authentic" reviews (the kill-risk)

**Decision:** Google = lawful **base/display layer + Google's own Grounding (Gemini) bootstrap**;
the **differentiator/moat = first-party UGC** ("by the people"), which you own and can run AI on
freely. The proprietary intelligence runs ONLY on owned content — never derived from Google's reviews.

Verified against current terms (Google Places policies updated 2026-06-17):
- Google Places returns ≤5 reviews, live only. **Cannot store review text** — only
  `place_id` (forever) and lat/lng (≤30 days). Must display author attribution + links.
  **No reviewer ethnicity/location/identity in the API.**
- Yelp Fusion: 3 review *excerpts* (~160 chars), attribution required, no storage.
- TripAdvisor: approved-partner-only, attribution required.

### Paths ruled OUT (and why) — do not revisit
- **Scrape + commingle reviews / hide the source** — violates every ToS (attribution
  required, caching/commingling prohibited) + copyright infringement (reviews are authored
  works) + App Store rejection. Strike entirely.
- **Filter by ethnicity from third-party reviews** — the signal does not exist in the data;
  inferring it (name/photo/language) is non-deterministic AND is processing "special category"
  data under GDPR Art. 9 / "sensitive information" under the Australian Privacy Act without
  consent. Unlawful regardless of accuracy.
- **Surname → ethnicity bucketing (East/South/SE Asian, etc.)** — real technique
  (e.g. Onomap) but: still special-category inference without consent (illegal), double-lossy
  (name→ethnicity→taste), and a PR landmine. Only lawful on consenting first-party users as an
  optional onboarding pre-fill they confirm.
- **Sort by reviewer's home location** — not a field the API returns; only obtainable by
  front-end profile scraping (ToS-prohibited, brittle, still sensitive data).
- **Gmail sign-in to "unlock" the user's Google reviews** — technically impossible: Google
  exposes no API to link a place's review author to your OAuth user (no join key), and consent
  from your user grants no rights over other reviewers' content or over Google's ToS.
- **★ Your OWN LLM run over Google reviews (even live, never stored)** — RETRACTED after legal
  research. Google Maps Platform Terms **§3.2.3(c) "No Creating Content"** is SEPARATE from the
  caching rule: you must not *create content based on* Google Maps Content. A live dish-consensus
  / expertise-score / summary derived from Google reviews is prohibited even if never stored.
  "Ephemeral" only answers caching, not this. The model brand is irrelevant (Gemini vs anything).
- **Multi-source aggregation (Google+Yelp+TripAdvisor+Michelin+OpenTable+IG/TikTok)** — does
  NOT fix the 5-review sample size; it multiplies the same derivative-works/anti-scraping wall
  across platforms, most of which have no lawful review-text API (Michelin/OpenTable: none;
  IG/TikTok: scraping aggressively enforced). No legal dense-corpus source exists.

**Why first-party UGC is the moat (only viable differentiator):** every shortcut to avoid
building your own review supply is blocked by law, ToS, or missing data. The differentiator
MUST be content you own. The flywheel is making people *want* to contribute (a product/growth
problem, not a data trick).

- **Creators:** a LIGHT, optional ingredient (lawful linked/embedded content) — NOT the

- **Deferred to v2/3:** self-declared-identity weighting, social/friends layer, moderation
  (Google relevance-sort is v1's de-facto moderation), privacy/consent model (v1 is single-player),

## AI pipeline + cost (LOCKED)

- **Two separate AI surfaces, by legality:**
  1. **Over Google data → ONLY via Google's Grounding with Google Maps (Gemini)** — the sole
     sanctioned channel; returns Google's grounded output + required source links; no training on
     it, no modifying/interspersing. Cheap (Gemini Flash class). Commodity feature, not a moat.
  2. **Over OWNED content (first-party reviews/tags) → model-agnostic layer**, swappable provider
     (Gemini Flash / DeepSeek; no Claude per founder). Dish-consensus, summaries, ranking run HERE.
- **Never run your own model over Google/third-party reviews** (see §3.2.3(c) above).
- **Cost is trivial at v1** (you + friends): Grounding + owned-content summaries are cents/month;
  a 24h ephemeral cache is a later optimization, not v1.
- **EUREKA — self-hosting to "save money" is a trap until scale:** Ollama is ~$200/mo for a GPU
  box at any volume; cheap hosted APIs cost cents at v1. Use hosted until ~100k restaurants/day.

## Stack (LOCKED so far)

- **Backend/DB: Supabase (managed Postgres)** + built-in auth + storage + RLS + pgvector.
  The calibrated/authenticity score is a relational aggregation → SQL, not NoSQL. **Scaling
  path is the same engine the whole way:** Supabase → dedicated instance + read replicas +
  Redis → self-managed Postgres / AWS Aurora at 100M. No rewrite, ever. (Firebase rejected:
  NoSQL fights the relational data; would force a rewrite at scale.)
- **Frontend / mobile: Expo / React Native (TypeScript)** — one codebase → iOS + Android +
  web; first-class Supabase SDK; best map/ecosystem fit; fastest solo iteration; OTA updates.
  (Flutter and PWA-first rejected: Dart ecosystem smaller; PWA weaker maps/camera and would
  be rebuilt natively later.)
- **Auth: Supabase Auth — email magic-link + Google OAuth.** Gmail sign-in = low-friction
  onboarding (NOT review-unlocking, which is impossible). Row-Level Security on every
  user-owned table.

## v1 Data Model (LOCKED — Postgres / Supabase)

```
profiles(id=auth uid, display_name, created_at; v2: home_city)
  profiles.suppressed_cuisines text[]            ← the "avoid Nepalese" filter (array; simple)
places(place_id PK Google, name, lat, lng[≤30d cache], last_seen)  ← NO Google reviews/photos stored
cuisines(id, name)                               ← normalized: the authenticity score queries this
place_cuisines(place_id FK, cuisine_id FK)       ← many-to-many
user_places(user_id FK, place_id FK, status enum['go','been','avoid'], UNIQUE(user_id,place_id))
reviews(id, user_id FK, place_id FK, body, rating, cuisine_id FK, created_at)   ← FIRST-PARTY only
review_photos(id, user_id FK, place_id FK, review_id FK?, dish_label, storage_path)  ← Supabase Storage
place_tags(id, place_id FK, tag, created_by FK, created_at)   ← community tags, first-party UGC
v2 reserve: follows(follower_id, followee_id) · profile_cuisines(profile_id, cuisine_id, level)
EPHEMERAL (not in Postgres): Google reviews · AI summaries · translations · expertise scores
```

Decisions: normalized `cuisines`/`place_cuisines` (joins beat array-scans for the score);
`suppressed_cuisines` as a plain array (simple filter); RLS everywhere (public read of
places/tags/reviews; write only your own rows).

## Legal constraints (carry into implementation; pre-scale lawyer check advised)
- Store ONLY `place_id` + first-party UGC. Never store Google/Yelp review text, photos, or
  AI summaries.
- Display attribution for all third-party reviews (author) and photos (minimal but present).
- Ethnicity/heritage data: first-party, opt-in, self-declared ONLY. Never inferred.
- Community "fact" tags (e.g. new management) are first-party UGC — lawful to store; get a
  real legal pass before relying on any review-derived extraction at scale. (No `plan-legal`
  skill exists in this environment — this needs a human lawyer pre-scale.)
